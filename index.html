Discover Skåne

Plan your perfect trip to Southern Sweden

Are you are looking to get back to nature, to enjoy a family beach break, or hoping to soak up some world class culture? Sweden's southern-most county has something for everyone. Find out below about some of our favourite places to visit, and see them marked on the map. Don't forget to get in touch with us to suggest more places of interest.

Lakes and Forests
Lakes

Even in the middle of the countryside, you are never far away from the water in Skåne. From the serene and unspoilt lakes that are deep in the forest, to those with beaches, watersports and angling, there really is something for everyone to enjoy. Three favourites of ours are lake Immeln, Hjälmsjön and Hammarsjön.

National Parks

The National Parks of Skåne are famous for their unspoilt beauty. The Sö‎deråsen and Stenshuvud National Parks are two of the most well known, and are perfect for hiking, as well as relaxing. They offer a real opportunity for visitors to get back to nature, and to 'switch off' from their busy lives.

Forests

Skåne is famous for its lush beech forests, as well as its pine forests. With wild mushrooms and blueberries in abundance, why not spend some time foraging whilst enjoying the beauty of nature. Skrylle, Häckeberga, and Fulltofta Nature Reserves are among the perfect places to get away from it all.

Beautiful Beaches
East Coast

Many of the finest beaches on the East Coast of Skåne are in the region of Österlen, which is famous for its unspoilt beauty. Kivik, Knäbäckshusen and Yngsjö all have beautiful beaches that are worth a visit.

West Coast

Most of the beaches on the West Coast of Skåne are situated within a reasonable driving distance of the cities of Malmö‎ and Helsingborg. This makes them perfect for anyone hoping to combine their beach holiday with a visit to the city. Our recommendations include Falsterbo, Farhultsbaden and Lomma norra badplats.

South Coast

The south coast of Skåne is home to the rocky beach at Smygehuk - which is at the southern-most point of Sweden. Other notable south-coast beaches include the sandy beaches of Sandhammaren and Ystad.

City and Culture
Malmö

Malmö is Sweden's third city, and is linked to Denmark by the world famous Öresunds bridge. It has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, and its many attractions include the Malmö Opera and Concert houses, Malmö Castle, and the Disgusting Food Museum.

View on map

Helsingborg

Helsingborg is situated on the west coast of Skåne, where the Öresund is at its narrowest. Denmark is clearly visible across the water. Although smaller than Malmö‎, it still has a thriving cultural scene. Dunkers Culture House, Helsingborg's Concert House and Sofiero Castle are just a few of the many places to visit here.

View on map

Lund

Home to the famous Lutheren Cathedral, Lund is thought to be the oldest city in modern-day Sweden. A vibrant student city, with cobbled streets, there is plenty to see and do. In addition to the cathedral, Kulturen - an outdoor museum, and the Lund planetarium are not to be missed.
